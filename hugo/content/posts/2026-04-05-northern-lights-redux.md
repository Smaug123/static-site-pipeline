# Thursday (2026-03-26)

Stay in Gatwick Airport (YOTEL) overnight.
Somehow they managed to make a hotel room feel exactly like a plane cabin.

{{< foldergallery src="images/galleries/Tromso2026/1-Outbound" summary="Trip to Gatwick and overnight there" >}}

# Friday (2026-03-27)

* Starbucks at Gatwick, Werthers Originals (couldn't face a Toblerone)
* Plane to Tromsø! Thanks Airbus (A320neo)
* Landed 1240 local time, skipped about 40mins of passport queue; thanks Article 28(1) of the EEA
* Bus into town ([Svipper](https://svipper.no/menu/travel/the-svipper-app/)); thanks Claude for telling me what to do here
* Lunch at [Pastafabrikken](https://www.pastafabrikken.no/); possibly even more expensive than central London, but the garlic bread was really good
* Incredible evening [aurora trip](https://auroratour.no/) (starting out with a vue panoramique at Skulsfjord while the sun set, then the best display at Lyfjord); thanks Sun, the lights were literally even visible while the sun was up

{{< foldergallery src="images/galleries/Tromso2026/2-NorthernLights" summary="Northern Lights trip" >}}

# Saturday (2026-03-28)

Quiet day really.

* Wander around town a bit, including over the huge bridge
* Tried to go up the cable car but it was shut
* Tried to go into [the cathedral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Troms%C3%B8_Cathedral) but it was, somehow, shut
* BICEP DAY in hotel gym
* Sauna in hotel

# Sunday (2026-03-29)

Ski-doo trip:

* 45min bus then 20min ferry
* Very cheerful Dutch guy leading the tour
* Met some tourists whose aurora tour on Friday got cancelled because they "were guaranteed not to see anything" :rip:
* The water closet really is one of the great triumphs of human civilisation and engineering

{{< foldergallery src="images/galleries/Tromso2026/4-Skidoo" >}}
